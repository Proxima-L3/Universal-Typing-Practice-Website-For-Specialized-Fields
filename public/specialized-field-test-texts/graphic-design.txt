Graphic design is the process of creating visual communication using typography, color, imagery, layout, composition, and digital tools to communicate messages, ideas, branding, and information across print and digital media. Graphic designers work on projects such as logos, advertisements, websites, packaging, social media graphics, posters, brochures, presentations, illustrations, motion graphics, and user interface designs. The field combines creativity, technical skill, visual problem-solving, and communication strategy to produce visually effective content.

A typical graphic design project begins with a creative brief outlining the client's goals, target audience, brand identity, deadlines, deliverables, and budget. Example project details:
Client: BrightWave Media
Project: Social Media Campaign
Deadline: 08/15/2026
Budget: $4,500
Deliverables: 12 Instagram Posts + 3 Banner Ads

Graphic designers often begin with brainstorming, mood boards, sketches, wireframes, and concept development before moving into digital production. Mood boards may include color palettes, typography samples, textures, imagery references, and layout inspiration.

Design software commonly used includes Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe InDesign, Figma, Canva, Sketch, Affinity Designer, and CorelDRAW. File formats often include:

.ai
.indd
.svg
.png
.jpg
.pdf
.eps

Design projects frequently involve layers, artboards, masks, clipping paths, guides, grids, and alignment tools. Example layer names:
- Background
- Header Text
- Logo

- Product Image

Typography is a major component of graphic design. Designers work with fonts, typefaces, kerning, tracking, leading, alignment, hierarchy, and readability. Common font categories include serif, sans-serif, script, display, and monospace.

Example typography settings:
Font: Helvetica Neue
Size: 24 pt
Tracking: +20
Leading: 28 pt
Alignment: Center

Common font pairings may include:
- Montserrat + Merriweather
- Futura + Garamond
- Open Sans + Playfair Display

Color theory is essential in design. Designers use RGB, CMYK, HEX, and Pantone color systems depending on output requirements.

Example color values:
RGB(255, 99, 71)
CMYK(0, 61, 72, 0)
HEX #FF6347
Pantone 186 C

RGB is typically used for digital screens, while CMYK is used for print production. Example conversion:
RGB Red = 255, 0, 0
CMYK Red = 0, 100, 100, 0

Graphic designers often use principles such as balance, contrast, alignment, repetition, proximity, hierarchy, white space, and emphasis to create effective layouts.

Example layout dimensions:
Instagram Post: 1080x1080 px
YouTube Thumbnail: 1280x720 px
Business Card: 3.5x2 inches
Poster Size: 24x36 inches

Resolution and image quality are critical. Digital graphics commonly use 72 PPI (pixels per inch), while print graphics require 300 DPI (dots per inch) for sharp output.

Example export settings:
Format: PNG
Resolution: 300 DPI
Color Mode: CMYK
Compression: High Quality

Branding projects often involve logo design, brand guidelines, typography systems, iconography, and visual identity standards.

Logo design may include vector artwork created using anchor points, bezier curves, paths, and shape tools. Vector graphics are scalable without quality loss and are commonly exported as .svg or .eps files.

Bleed: 0.125 inches

- Readable font sizes
