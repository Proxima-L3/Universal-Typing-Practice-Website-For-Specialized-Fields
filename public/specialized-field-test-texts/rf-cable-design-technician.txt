An RF cable design technician works with radio frequency transmission systems, coaxial cable assemblies, connectors, shielding systems, impedance matching, signal integrity, and communication hardware used in telecommunications, aerospace, defense, broadcasting, networking, wireless infrastructure, and electronic testing environments. The role involves designing, assembling, testing, troubleshooting, documenting, and maintaining RF cable systems that transmit high-frequency electrical signals with minimal loss and interference.

RF stands for Radio Frequency, which refers to electromagnetic signals typically ranging from 3 kHz to 300 GHz. RF cable systems are used in antennas, satellite communications, Wi-Fi equipment, radar systems, cellular towers, GPS devices, laboratory instrumentation, and military communication systems.

A typical RF cable assembly may include coaxial cable, SMA connectors, BNC connectors, TNC connectors, N-type connectors, heat shrink tubing, ferrite beads, shielding braid, grounding hardware, and labeling materials.

Cable assembly procedures often involve stripping insulation, trimming dielectric material, soldering center conductors, crimping ferrules, applying heat shrink tubing, and inspecting connector alignment.

Example assembly checklist:
1. Measure cable length
2. Strip outer jacket
3. Trim braid and dielectric
4. Install connector body
5. Solder center pin
6. Crimp ferrule
7. Perform continuity test

RF technicians commonly use crimp tools, torque wrenches, cable cutters, soldering stations, wire strippers, heat guns, and microscopes during cable assembly.
